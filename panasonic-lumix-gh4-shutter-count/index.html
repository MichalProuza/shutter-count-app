Panasonic Lumix GH4 — Shutter Rating

The Panasonic Lumix GH4 (DMC-GH4, 2014) was a landmark in consumer video history — the first interchangeable-lens camera to offer internal 4K/UHD video recording. Featuring a 16.05 MP Micro Four Thirds Digital Live MOS sensor, 12 fps burst shooting, weather sealing, and the optional YAGH audio/video interface unit, the GH4 defined the hybrid photography/videography market for years. Its mechanical shutter is rated at 150,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Panasonic Lumix GH4

  1. Via camera menu: Go to MENU → Setup (wrench icon) tab → scroll to Shutter Count or Body Battery Info. The total shutter actuation count is displayed here on all GH4 firmware versions.
  2. Via ExifTool: Install ExifTool and run exiftool -ShutterCount yourfile.RW2 in a terminal. Some GH4 firmware versions write a shot count into Panasonic-specific MakerNote tags, though this is not guaranteed on all firmware releases.
  3. Via Lumix Sync app: Connect the GH4 to the Lumix Sync smartphone app via Wi-Fi. Camera information including total shot count is accessible in the app's device status panel.
  4. When buying used, ask the seller for a live demonstration of the camera menu shutter count screen or for a short video of the menu. Be cautious of sellers who claim the count is inaccessible.

Panasonic Lumix GH4 — FAQ

What is the rated shutter life of the Panasonic Lumix GH4?

The GH4 mechanical shutter is rated at 150,000 actuations. Successor models (GH5, GH5S, GH5 II, GH6) are rated at 200,000.

Does video recording wear out the GH4 shutter?

No. Video recording on the GH4 uses the sensor's electronic rolling readout. The mechanical focal-plane shutter only operates when taking still photos in mechanical shutter mode. This is why video-heavy GH4 bodies can have extremely low mechanical shutter counts despite years of regular professional video use.

What should I check when buying a used GH4?

Beyond shutter count: sensor cleanliness (shoot a grey wall at f/11 and check for dust), IBIS function, card slot condition (SD UHS-I and 2nd slot), battery health (check charge hold), rubber seals on ports (GH4 is splash-dust resistant), and ensure all control dials and buttons respond correctly. Also test the 4K recording functionality as the primary reason the GH4 is still sought after.

Is the GH4 still relevant in 2026?

The GH4 produces clean, usable 4K footage and supports log profiles for colour grading. For a budget video or B-roll camera, it remains capable. Its 16 MP sensor and contrast-detect AF (no phase detection) have been surpassed by newer bodies, but for run-and-gun video on a tight budget the GH4 is excellent value used.
