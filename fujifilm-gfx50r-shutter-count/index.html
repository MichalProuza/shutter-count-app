Fujifilm GFX 50R — Shutter Ratings

The GFX 50R is Fujifilm's rangefinder-style medium format mirrorless camera, sharing its 51.4 MP sensor with the GFX 50S in a more compact, travel-oriented body. Knowing the shutter count is essential when buying or selling a used GFX 50R, as medium format shutter replacements are significantly more expensive than APS-C or full-frame bodies.

Note: Fujifilm does not publish an official shutter rating for the GFX 50R. The ~150,000 figure is an industry estimate consistent with other GFX bodies. Medium format cameras are often used more deliberately than APS-C cameras, so many used GFX 50R bodies have relatively low shutter counts.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Fujifilm GFX 50R

The GFX 50R stores its shutter count in the MakerNote section of every RAF file it produces (tag 0x1438, ImageCount). You can read it instantly with ShutterCount:

  1. Take any photo with your GFX 50R and locate the resulting .RAF file on your memory card or in your transfers folder.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge).
  3. Drag the RAF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count appears instantly. No upload, no account, no plugins required.
Important: Formatting the memory card in the GFX 50R resets the in-file counter stored in RAF MakerNote tag 0x1438. If the counter shows an unexpectedly low number on a used camera, ask to see the original unformatted card or use an alternative verification method.

GFX 50R vs GFX 50S — Shutter Count Differences

The GFX 50R and GFX 50S share the same 51.4 MP medium format CMOS sensor and use an identical RAF file structure. For shutter count purposes, they are equivalent: both store the count in MakerNote tag 0x1438.

The key physical difference is the body style. The GFX 50S uses a traditional SLR-inspired body with a large integrated grip. The GFX 50R uses a rangefinder-style horizontal body — smaller, lighter, and better suited for street or travel photography. The shutter mechanism is the same unit with the same estimated lifespan.

When buying used, the GFX 50R often commands a slight price premium for its body ergonomics in street and travel contexts, despite identical image quality. Always verify the shutter count before purchasing either model.

Does the electronic shutter affect the count?

No. When shooting in electronic shutter mode, the GFX 50R reads the sensor electrically without moving the mechanical curtain. These frames add zero to the mechanical shutter count. Only mechanical shutter exposures are counted.

Fujifilm GFX 50R Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Fujifilm GFX 50R?

Take a photo with your GFX 50R, then drop the resulting RAF file into shuttercount.app. The tool reads MakerNote tag 0x1438 from the RAF file in your browser — no upload required.

What is the rated shutter life of the GFX 50R?

Fujifilm does not publish an official shutter rating for the GFX 50R. The estimated lifespan is approximately 150,000 actuations, consistent with the GFX 50S and other GFX bodies. Individual shutters frequently exceed this estimate.

Why does the counter reset after formatting my card?

The ImageCount tag stored in RAF MakerNote (0x1438) is written per image and is independent from the camera's internal hardware counter. Formatting the card resets the in-file counter. The camera's internal counter (accessible via USB or camera menu) is not affected by formatting.
