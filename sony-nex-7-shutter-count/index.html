Sony NEX-7 — Shutter Rating & Context

The Sony NEX-7 (2011) was the enthusiast flagship of Sony's NEX line — a compact mirrorless camera built around a class-leading 24.3 MP APS-C sensor at a time when most mirrorless cameras offered 12–16 MP. Its triple-dial control layout (shared with the later A7 series), built-in OLED EVF, and best-in-class image quality made it a favourite among serious photographers looking for a DSLR-quality image in a pocketable body. Sony does not publish a rated shutter count for the NEX-7; field experience estimates approximately 100,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Sony NEX-7

  1. Take a still photo with your Sony NEX-7 and locate the .ARW file on your memory card.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the ARW file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The mechanical shutter count appears instantly, decrypted from MakerNote tag 0x9050 in your browser.

The NEX-7 does not have a silent/electronic shutter mode. All still frames increment the mechanical shutter counter, making the ARW reading a reliable record of total actuations.

How Does ShutterCount Read the Sony NEX-7 Shutter Count?

The Sony NEX-7 stores the mechanical shutter count in ARW MakerNote EXIF tag 0x9050. Like all Sony Alpha/NEX cameras, the value is encrypted using a cubic residue cipher (modulo 249). ShutterCount decrypts and reads this value entirely in your browser — no server, no upload.

100% local: All processing happens in your browser. The ARW file never leaves your device.

Can I use a JPEG instead of an ARW file?

JPEGs from the NEX-7 do not contain the encrypted MakerNote data needed to read the shutter count. Always use the original .ARW raw file for an accurate reading.

Sony NEX-7 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Sony NEX-7?

Shoot an ARW frame and drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is decrypted from MakerNote tag 0x9050 in your browser, instantly and privately.

Is the Sony NEX-7 still worth buying?

For photography on a tight budget, the NEX-7 offers a genuinely excellent 24.3 MP APS-C sensor with strong dynamic range. It lacks IBIS, 4K video, touch screen, and fast continuous AF — all standard on modern Sony cameras. As a studio or slow-paced street camera for an experienced shooter, it remains capable. Expect to find clean used bodies for well under £/€/$200.

Can the Sony NEX-7 shutter count be reset?

Only Sony authorised service centres can reset the hardware counter. Always verify shutter count from an original ARW file — not a screenshot or JPEG.

Does the NEX-7 work with modern Sony E-mount lenses?

Yes — all Sony E-mount lenses (including full-frame FE lenses) physically mount on the NEX-7. Full-frame lenses are used in APS-C crop mode. Modern AF features like subject tracking and real-time Eye AF are not available on this body, but basic phase-detect and contrast AF work with native lenses.
