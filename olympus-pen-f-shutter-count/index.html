Olympus PEN-F — Shutter Rating & Premium Rangefinder Context

The Olympus PEN-F (2016) is the highest-specification body in Olympus's PEN line and the only PEN camera with a built-in electronic viewfinder. It features a 20.3 MP Micro Four Thirds sensor — the highest resolution Olympus offered at the time — 5-axis in-body image stabilisation, a 2.36M-dot EVF, a tilting 3-inch touchscreen, and a unique dual Creative Control dial interface. Its mechanical shutter is rated at approximately 200,000 actuations.

Dropping a PEN-F ORF file into shuttercount.app returns EXIF metadata (camera model, settings, focal length) but not the actuation count. Use the camera menu (Setup → Camera Information) for the reliable count.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Olympus PEN-F

  1. Via camera menu: Press MENU → navigate to the Setup tab (spanner/wrench icon) → scroll to Camera Information → the shutter count (total actuations) is displayed here.
  2. Via ExifTool: Install ExifTool and run exiftool -ShutterCount yourfile.ORF. Some PEN-F firmware versions embed a shot count in the Olympus MakerNote, though this is not consistently available across all firmware.
  3. When buying used, ask the seller to demonstrate the camera menu shutter count on-screen or provide a short video showing the Camera Information screen. A seller who cannot or will not show the count is a red flag.

Olympus PEN-F Shutter Count — FAQ

What is the rated shutter life of the Olympus PEN-F?

The PEN-F mechanical shutter is rated at approximately 200,000 actuations, consistent with Olympus's other prosumer OM-D bodies of the same era.

Can I check the shutter count from an ORF file?

Not reliably. Olympus ORF files do not contain a standardised confirmed shutter-count field. The camera menu (Setup → Camera Information) is the primary and most reliable method for checking the PEN-F's actuation count.

What makes the Olympus PEN-F unique among PEN cameras?

The PEN-F is the only PEN-series body with a built-in EVF (2.36M dots), 5-axis IBIS, and a 20.3 MP sensor — the highest resolution Olympus released for Micro Four Thirds at the time of its launch. Its dual Creative Control dials (colour tone and monochrome effect) are unique in the Olympus lineup. It was discontinued without a direct successor.

Is the PEN-F still worth buying in 2026?

For street photographers and M4/3 enthusiasts who value the compact rangefinder form factor and premium build, the PEN-F remains excellent used-market value. Its 20 MP sensor, IBIS, and built-in EVF set it above any other PEN body. Verify LCD hinge rigidity, dial function, and battery health when inspecting used examples.

How does the PEN-F compare to the OM-D E-M5 Mark II?

The E-M5 Mark II has a similar 16.1 MP sensor (lower resolution), weather sealing (the PEN-F is not weather sealed), and a more traditional DSLR-inspired body shape. The PEN-F offers higher 20 MP resolution, a more compact rangefinder design, and the Creative Control dials. For rough-weather shooting, the E-M5 II is more suitable; for resolution and design, the PEN-F has the edge.
