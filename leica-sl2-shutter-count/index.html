Leica SL2 & SL2-S — Shutter Ratings

The Leica SL2 is Leica's professional full-frame L-mount mirrorless camera, launched in 2019. Its 47.3 MP BSI-CMOS sensor, IP54 weather sealing, and native DNG output make it a camera of choice for studio, landscape, and high-end commercial photographers. The SL2-S (2020) uses a 24.6 MP sensor optimised for low light and video production.

Model Release Sensor Est. Shutter Life RAW Format
Leica SL2 2019 47.3 MP full-frame BSI-CMOS ~150,000–200,000 DNG
Leica SL2-S 2020 24.6 MP full-frame BSI-CMOS ~150,000–200,000 DNG
Note: Leica does not publish official shutter ratings. The SL2-S is frequently used as a video camera — video uses the electronic shutter and adds zero mechanical actuations. A used SL2-S with heavy video use may have a very low mechanical shutter count, which is a positive indicator, not a concern.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Leica SL2

The Leica SL2 and SL2-S produce native DNG files containing full EXIF metadata, including the shutter count in MakerNote tags. ShutterCount reads this directly in your browser:

  1. Take any photo with your Leica SL2 or SL2-S and locate the resulting .DNG file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge).
  3. Drag the DNG file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count appears instantly. No upload, no account, no plugins required.

Alternatively, use ExifTool: exiftool -ShutterCount image.dng

When buying used: Ask the seller to take a fresh photo in front of you and hand over the DNG file directly from the card. This ensures the count shown is the current count, not a pre-selected low-count file.

SL2 vs SL2-S — Shutter Count Considerations

Same shutter mechanism, different use cases

The SL2 (47.3 MP) is primarily a stills camera; the SL2-S (24.6 MP) is used heavily for video. Since video on the SL2-S uses the electronic shutter, a high-video-use SL2-S body will typically have a much lower mechanical shutter count than a comparable SL2 used primarily for stills. This is an advantage when buying: a heavily used SL2-S for video is likely to have a very fresh mechanical shutter.

L-mount compatibility

Both the SL2 and SL2-S use the L-mount, shared with Panasonic (Lumix S1, S5, etc.) and Sigma (fp, fp L). All L-mount cameras from all three brands are fully lens-compatible.

Leica SL2 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check shutter count on the Leica SL2?

Drop a DNG file from your SL2 into shuttercount.app. The shutter count is read from the file's EXIF data in your browser — no upload required.

Does electronic shutter or video use affect the count?

No. Only mechanical shutter exposures are counted. Video recording and electronic shutter shots add zero to the mechanical counter.

Is Leica shutter replacement expensive?

Yes. Leica camera service is handled exclusively through authorised Leica service centres, and costs are significantly higher than for Japanese camera brands. Budget €400–800 or more for a shutter replacement, depending on the service centre and model.
