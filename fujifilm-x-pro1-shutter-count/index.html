Fujifilm X-Pro1 — The Original X-Trans

The Fujifilm X-Pro1 (2012) is a landmark camera. It introduced the unique X-Trans CMOS sensor design (eliminating the optical low-pass filter via a non-Bayer colour array), launched the Fujifilm X-mount with three superb prime lenses, and brought back the rangefinder-style hybrid viewfinder in a digital body. Aimed at photojournalists and street photographers, it became the foundation for everything that followed in the X-series — including today's X-Pro3, X-T5 and X100VI.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Fujifilm X-Pro1

The X-Pro1 does not display the shutter count in its menus. The count is embedded in every RAF RAW file.

  1. Take any photo with your Fujifilm X-Pro1 and locate the .RAF file on the SD card.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the RAF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count appears instantly. No upload, no account, no plugins required.

Fujifilm X-Pro1 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Fujifilm X-Pro1?

Shoot a RAF file and drop it into shuttercount.app. The shutter count is read from the Fujifilm MakerNote in your browser — no upload needed.

What is the rated shutter life of the X-Pro1?

Fujifilm never published an official figure. Most working units achieve around 100,000 actuations before any service intervention.

Is the X-Pro1 still worth buying in 2026?

For its character, yes. The X-Trans I sensor produces a distinctive rendering that many photographers still chase, and the rangefinder-style body remains a delight to shoot. If you need fast autofocus or video, look at a newer X-series body.

What is the difference between the X-Pro1 and X-Pro2?

The X-Pro2 (2016) upgraded to a 24 MP X-Trans III sensor, added dual SD slots, weather sealing, a much faster autofocus system, and improvements to the hybrid viewfinder. The X-Pro1 is slower in every operational sense but retains a more deliberate, contemplative shooting feel.

Can the X-Pro1 shutter count be reset?

Only Fujifilm authorised service centres can reset the hardware counter after a shutter replacement. Always verify from an original RAF file.
