Panasonic Lumix S1 — Shutter Rating

The Panasonic Lumix S1 (2019) was Panasonic's first full-frame L-mount mirrorless camera, co-developed with Leica and Sigma as part of the L-Mount Alliance. It pairs a 24.2 MP full-frame Dual Native ISO sensor with a professional-grade body featuring extensive weather sealing (IP53 equivalent), dual card slots (CFexpress B / SD), and a best-in-class 5-axis IBIS system rated at 6.5 stops. The mechanical shutter is rated at a remarkably high 400,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Panasonic Lumix S1

Important: Panasonic does not embed the shutter count in RW2 RAW files. The ShutterCount browser tool cannot read the S1 count from a file drop. Use the camera menu method below.

Method 1: Camera Menu (recommended)

  1. Power on your Panasonic Lumix S1.
  2. Press the MENU button and navigate to the Setup menu (wrench icon).
  3. Scroll to find Shutter Count (may appear as "Shutter Actuations" in some firmware versions).
  4. The current mechanical shutter count is displayed on screen.

Method 2: Lumix Sync app

Connect your S1 to the Panasonic Lumix Sync smartphone app via Wi-Fi. The app displays the shutter count under the camera information section.

Method 3: ExifTool (advanced)

Some firmware versions embed a partial shutter count in RW2 EXIF data under PanasonicRaw:ShutterCount. You can try: exiftool -ShutterCount your_file.RW2 — however, Panasonic does not officially guarantee this value is always present or current.

Panasonic Lumix S1 Shutter Count — FAQ

What is the rated shutter life of the Panasonic Lumix S1?

The Panasonic Lumix S1 mechanical shutter is rated at 400,000 actuations — one of the highest ratings among full-frame mirrorless cameras and on par with professional DSLRs like the Nikon D6.

Why can't I check the S1 shutter count from a RW2 file?

Panasonic does not consistently embed the mechanical shutter count in RW2 RAW files, unlike Canon (CR3), Nikon (NEF), Sony (ARW), and Fujifilm (RAF). The authoritative source is the camera's own menu counter.

What is the difference between the Lumix S1 and S1R?

The S1R uses a 47.3 MP sensor for maximum resolution, while the S1 uses 24.2 MP for better high-ISO performance and faster continuous shooting. Both share the same body, 400,000-actuation shutter, and weather sealing. The S1 received a firmware update adding 4K/60p and V-Log; the S1R also supports High Resolution Shot (96 MP composite).
