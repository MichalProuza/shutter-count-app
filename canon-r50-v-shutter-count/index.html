How to Check Shutter Count on the Canon R50 V

The R50 V does not show the image count in its on-screen menus. The counter is embedded in every CR3 RAW file.

  1. Take any photo with your Canon R50 V and locate the .CR3 file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the CR3 file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The image count appears instantly. No upload, no account, no plugins required.

For the most accurate count, use a file shot before formatting the card, as formatting may reset the ImageCount field.

Canon has not published an official shutter rating for the R50 V. As an entry-level body, use conservative thresholds.

How Does ShutterCount Read the R50 V Shutter Count?

The R50 V writes images in Canon's CR3 format. ShutterCount parses the ISOBMFF box hierarchy to find the EXIF MakerNote and reads the ImageCount field at byte offset 0x086D as a uint16 little-endian value.

This is the same offset and field type used by the Canon EOS R1 and R6 Mark III. It differs from the uint32 offset 0x0D29 used by the original R50.

100% local: All processing happens in your browser. The CR3 file never leaves your device.

Does the electronic shutter count?

No. The R50 V's electronic shutter does not engage the mechanical curtain and does not increment the ImageCount.

Canon R50 V Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Canon EOS R50 V?

Shoot a CR3 RAW file and drop it into shuttercount.app. The ImageCount is read from offset 0x086D in your browser — no upload needed.

Does the R50 V shutter count reset on format?

The ImageCount field (uint16 at 0x086D) may reset when the camera is formatted. For the most reliable reading, check a file taken before formatting.

What is different from the original R50?

The R50 stores its shutter count as a uint32 at offset 0x0D29, while the R50 V uses a uint16 ImageCount at 0x086D. The R50 V shares this counter type with the R1 and R6 Mark III. The R50 V also adds improved video features and a fully articulating screen.
