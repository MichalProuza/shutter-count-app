Nikon D200 — Shutter Rating & Overview

The Nikon D200 (2005) was a landmark prosumer DSLR that brought semi-professional features to an accessible price point. With its 10.2 MP CCD sensor, magnesium-alloy weather-sealed body, 11-point Multi-CAM 1000 autofocus, 5 fps burst rate, and 100% viewfinder coverage option (with the MB-D200 grip), it became one of the most respected DX-format bodies of its generation. The CCD sensor is particularly prized for its colour rendition. The mechanical shutter is rated at 100,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Nikon D200

  1. Take a still photo with your Nikon D200 and locate the .NEF file on the CF card.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the NEF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The mechanical shutter count appears instantly, read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser.

The D200 uses a mechanical shutter for all exposures — every photo increments the counter.

How Does ShutterCount Read the Nikon D200 Shutter Count?

The Nikon D200 stores the mechanical shutter count in NEF MakerNote EXIF tag 0x00A7 (decimal 167). This is the universal Nikon shutter count field, used identically across all Nikon DSLRs and Z-series mirrorless cameras. ShutterCount reads this tag directly in your browser — no encryption or special processing is needed.

Nikon D200 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Nikon D200?

Shoot a NEF frame and drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser, instantly and privately.

What is the rated shutter life of the Nikon D200?

Nikon rates the D200 at 100,000 actuations. This was the standard prosumer rating of the era, shared with the D100 before it.

What made the D200 special?

The D200 was Nikon’s first truly affordable semi-pro body — a weather-sealed, magnesium-alloy DX DSLR with professional build quality and controls at a fraction of the D2X price. Its 10.2 MP CCD sensor produced exceptionally pleasing colour, and it remains a cult favourite among film-to-digital converts.

Does the D200 use CF or SD cards?

The D200 uses a single CompactFlash (CF Type I/II) card slot. You’ll need a CF card reader to transfer NEF files to a computer for shutter count checking.

Can the Nikon D200 shutter count be reset?

Only Nikon authorised service centres can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original NEF file, not a screenshot or JPEG.
