How to Check Shutter Count on the Pentax K-3 Mark III

  1. Via ExifTool (most reliable): Install ExifTool and run exiftool -ShutterCount yourfile.PEF. Pentax embeds the shutter count in the Pentax MakerNote of every PEF file. This method is reliable across firmware versions.
  2. Via camera menu: Navigate to MENU → Set-up tab → Camera Info. The total shutter count is displayed here alongside other camera statistics.
  3. Via Pentax Image Transmitter 2: Connect the K-3 Mark III via USB to a PC running Pentax's Image Transmitter 2 software. Camera information including shutter count is accessible in the software's camera status panel.
  4. When buying used, ask the seller for a live demonstration of the Camera Info menu screen or for a sample PEF file you can run through ExifTool yourself.

Pentax K-3 Mark III — FAQ

What is the rated shutter life of the Pentax K-3 Mark III?

The K-3 Mark III shutter is rated at approximately 300,000 actuations. This is significantly higher than competing APS-C DSLRs (typically 100,000–200,000). The high rating reflects Pentax's engineering focus on shutter durability in their pro-grade bodies.

Can ExifTool read the shutter count from Pentax PEF files?

Yes. Pentax stores the shutter count in a MakerNote tag within PEF files. ExifTool reliably extracts this with exiftool -ShutterCount file.PEF. This is the recommended method for verifying the count on a used K-3 Mark III. If the seller saves in DNG format, the Pentax MakerNote may not be present in the DNG — request a PEF sample.

How does the K-3 Mark III compare to the K-3 II and K-3?

The K-3 Mark III introduced a completely new 25.73 MP sensor (vs 24.35 MP in K-3/K-3 II), a redesigned body with a deeper grip, improved optical viewfinder with 0.95x magnification, built-in GPS (versus optional accessory in predecessors), Astrotracer II, and significantly enhanced PDAF coverage. The shutter rating (~300,000) is consistent across all three generations.

What should I check besides shutter count on a used K-3 Mark III?

Inspect: IBIS/SR function (test at slow shutter speeds), 92-point weather sealing gasket condition, GPS functionality, optical viewfinder clarity, the dual SD/CF express card slot operation, and battery health (D-LI90P). Given the K-3 Mark III's popularity with wildlife and astro photographers, check for sensor dust and inspect the front element area for any impact marks.
