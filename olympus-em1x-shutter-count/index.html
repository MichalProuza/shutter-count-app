Olympus OM-D E-M1X — Shutter Rating

The Olympus OM-D E-M1X is a 2019 professional Micro Four Thirds camera in a large, dual-grip body with a built-in vertical grip — essentially a standalone camera body equivalent to attaching a battery grip. Designed for sports, wildlife, and aviation photographers, the E-M1X features dual TruePic VIII+ processors, 60 fps Pro Capture, 18 fps mechanical burst, and an IP53-rated body. Olympus rates its mechanical shutter at 400,000 actuations — matching the OM-1 flagship.

Pro Capture and the shutter count: The E-M1X's Pro Capture mode uses the electronic shutter to buffer frames before the shutter is pressed — these electronic frames add zero mechanical wear. Only the subsequent mechanical burst (up to 18 fps) increments the counter. Wildlife photographers who heavily use Pro Capture electronic pre-burst may find their E-M1X has a much lower mechanical count than expected for its age.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Olympus OM-D E-M1X

ORF limitation: Olympus ORF files do not reliably store the mechanical shutter count in EXIF data. The in-file count in ORF MakerNote tags is undocumented and firmware-dependent. The camera menu is the most reliable method.

Method 1 — Camera Menu (most reliable)

  1. Power on the E-M1X and press the Menu button.
  2. Navigate to the Setup Menu (wrench icon tab).
  3. Scroll to find Number of Shutter Releases.
  4. The total mechanical shutter actuation count is displayed.

Method 2 — Browser Tool (attempt)

  1. Take any photo with your E-M1X and locate the .ORF file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app and drag the ORF onto the drop zone.
  3. If the firmware embeds the count in a readable MakerNote tag, it will appear. If not, the tool will note that no confirmed shutter count tag was found — use Method 1.

Olympus OM-D E-M1X Shutter Count — FAQ

Does the Olympus E-M1X store shutter count in ORF files?

Not reliably. Olympus ORF MakerNote data includes various image-related fields, but the mechanical shutter count is not documented in a confirmed, stable tag. Use the camera's Setup Menu for the most accurate reading.

Is the E-M1X a good used camera in 2025?

Yes, for wildlife and sports photography with M4/3 lenses. The 400,000-actuation shutter, Pro Capture mode, and dual-grip build make it durable for continued professional use. The main limitation compared to newer OM System bodies is the autofocus system, which lacks the AI subject-recognition AF of the OM-1 line.

How does 18 fps burst affect shutter count wear on the E-M1X?

At 18 fps mechanical burst, a single 10-second wildlife sequence adds 180 actuations. A full day of active wildlife shooting at 18 fps can add 5,000–10,000 actuations easily. Checking the count is especially important for sports/wildlife E-M1X bodies.
