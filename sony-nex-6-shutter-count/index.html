Sony NEX-6 — Shutter Rating & Context

The Sony NEX-6 (2012) was Sony's mid-range NEX mirrorless camera, featuring a 16.1 MP APS-C sensor, a built-in electronic viewfinder (EVF), and an innovative hybrid autofocus system combining 25 phase-detect and 99 contrast-detect points. It was one of the first mirrorless cameras to offer built-in Wi-Fi for wireless image transfer. Sony does not publish an official shutter rating for the NEX-6; field experience puts the estimated lifespan at approximately 100,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Sony NEX-6

  1. Take a still photo with your Sony NEX-6 and locate the .ARW file on your memory card.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the ARW file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The mechanical shutter count appears instantly, decrypted from MakerNote tag 0x9050 in your browser.

The NEX-6 does not have a silent/electronic shutter mode. Every still frame increments the mechanical shutter counter, making the ARW count an accurate record of total usage.

How Does ShutterCount Read the Sony NEX-6 Shutter Count?

The Sony NEX-6 stores the mechanical shutter count in ARW MakerNote EXIF tag 0x9050. Like all Sony Alpha/NEX cameras, the value is encrypted using a cubic residue cipher (modulo 249). ShutterCount decrypts and reads this value entirely in your browser — no server, no upload.

100% local: All processing happens in your browser. The ARW file never leaves your device.

Can I use a JPEG instead of an ARW file?

JPEGs from the NEX-6 do not contain the encrypted MakerNote data needed to read the shutter count reliably. Always use the original .ARW raw file for an accurate reading.

Sony NEX-6 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Sony NEX-6?

Shoot an ARW frame and drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is decrypted from MakerNote tag 0x9050 in your browser, instantly and privately.

What is the rated shutter life of the Sony NEX-6?

Sony does not publish an official shutter rating for the NEX-6. Field experience suggests approximately 100,000 actuations as a realistic estimate. Many bodies have exceeded this.

Is the Sony NEX-6 still a good camera?

For stills photography on a tight budget, the NEX-6 offers a capable 16.1 MP APS-C sensor and fast hybrid AF. Its Wi-Fi, OLED EVF, and compact size remain strong points. Key limitations include no 4K video, limited native lens selection (the E-mount ecosystem has grown substantially since 2012, but many modern lenses are large), and no IBIS.

Can the Sony NEX-6 shutter count be reset?

Only Sony authorised service centres can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original ARW file — not a screenshot or JPEG which can be fabricated or won't contain the correct MakerNote data.
