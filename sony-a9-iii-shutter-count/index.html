Sony A9 III — Shutter Rating & Global Shutter

The Sony A9 III is the world's first full-frame mirrorless camera with a true global shutter sensor. Released in 2023, it offers 120 fps burst shooting, 1/80,000 s flash sync, and zero rolling shutter distortion. Despite its revolutionary electronic capabilities, it retains a mechanical shutter rated at 500,000 actuations.

Important caveat for A9 III buyers: The A9 III's global electronic shutter is its primary selling point for sports and wildlife photography — many users shoot almost exclusively in electronic mode. This means the mechanical shutter count readable from ARW files may be far lower than the total number of frames the camera has taken. A low mechanical count on an A9 III does not necessarily mean the camera has seen light use.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Sony A9 III

  1. Take a photo with your Sony A9 III using the mechanical shutter (not the electronic/silent mode). Locate the .ARW file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the ARW file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The mechanical shutter count appears instantly. No upload, no account required.

If you typically shoot in electronic mode, shoot one dedicated mechanical frame to capture the current count.

How Does ShutterCount Read the A9 III Shutter Count?

The A9 III stores the mechanical shutter count encrypted in ARW MakerNote tag 0x9050. ShutterCount applies Sony's cubic residue decryption (modulo 249) and reads the 32-bit count from byte offset 0x003a — confirmed for the A9 III alongside the A7 IV, A7 III, and A1.

Does the global electronic shutter count?

No. The A9 III's global shutter reads all pixels simultaneously without any mechanical curtain movement. Those frames are not counted in the ARW MakerNote. This is the most significant caveat of all current mirrorless cameras: the A9 III's mechanical count can be dramatically lower than total frames shot.

What does a very low count on an A9 III really mean?

It could mean the camera is genuinely lightly used, or it could mean the owner primarily used electronic shutter mode (extremely common for sports photographers). When buying a used A9 III, also consider cosmetic wear, buffer behaviour, and autofocus calibration as indicators of overall usage.

Sony A9 III Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Sony A9 III?

Shoot a mechanical ARW frame, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is decrypted from tag 0x9050 entirely in your browser.

What is the rated shutter life of the Sony A9 III?

Sony rates the A9 III mechanical shutter at 500,000 actuations.

Is 10,000 actuations normal for a used A9 III?

Possibly. A sports photographer who shoots 5,000 frames a day mostly in electronic mode could own a camera for months and only have a few thousand mechanical actuations. A very low count on an A9 III is less meaningful as an indicator of light use than on other cameras.

Can the A9 III shutter count be reset or faked?

Only a Sony authorised service centre can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original ARW file.
