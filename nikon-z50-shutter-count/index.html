Nikon Z50 — Shutter Rating

The Nikon Z50 is Nikon's 2019 first APS-C mirrorless camera in the Z-mount system. With a 20.9 MP DX-format CMOS sensor, 11 fps burst shooting, and a compact body, the Z50 became a popular choice for enthusiasts and travel photographers. It features a generous 200,000-actuation shutter rating — surprisingly higher than its 2024 successor, the Z50 II (100,000).

How to Check Shutter Count on the Nikon Z50

  1. Take any photo with your Nikon Z50 and locate the .NEF file on your memory card or computer.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the NEF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count and remaining life percentage appear instantly. No upload, no account required.

How Does ShutterCount Read the Z50 Shutter Count?

Like all Nikon Z-series bodies, the Z50 stores the shutter count in MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in every NEF file. ShutterCount reads this tag universally across all Nikon bodies — no model-specific configuration required.

All processing happens locally in your browser. The NEF file is never transmitted anywhere.

Nikon Z50 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Nikon Z50?

Shoot a NEF RAW file, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser.

What is the rated shutter life of the Nikon Z50?

Nikon rates the Z50 at 200,000 actuations — generous for an APS-C entry-level body and double the Z50 II's rating.

Is 80,000 actuations a lot for a Nikon Z50?

At 80,000 actuations, the Z50 has used 40% of its rated 200,000-shot lifespan — moderate use. There is still significant life remaining, and the price should reflect its used condition fairly.

Can the shutter count on a Nikon Z50 be reset or faked?

Only a Nikon authorised service centre can reset the hardware counter after a physical shutter replacement. Always verify from an original NEF file, not a screenshot.
