Nikon D850 — Shutter Rating & DSLR Context

The Nikon D850 (2017) is widely regarded as one of the finest DSLRs ever made. Its 45.7 MP back-illuminated full-frame sensor delivers exceptional dynamic range and resolution across a 14-stop latitude, enabling photographers to make large prints rivalling medium format. At 9fps (with optional MB-D18 grip and EN-EL18 battery), it is also among the fastest high-resolution DSLRs ever built. The mechanical shutter is rated at 200,000 actuations. It remains in active use by many working photographers and has a very healthy used market.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Nikon D850

  1. Take a still photo with your Nikon D850 and locate the .NEF file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the NEF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The mechanical shutter count appears instantly, read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser.

The D850 uses a conventional focal-plane mechanical shutter for all shooting — there is no electronic shutter mode, so every frame increments the counter.

Nikon D850 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Nikon D850?

Shoot a NEF frame and drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser, instantly and privately.

What is the rated shutter life of the Nikon D850?

Nikon rates the D850 at 200,000 actuations. Many D850 shutters exceed this figure significantly with proper maintenance, but the failure risk increases after the rated limit.

Is the Nikon D850 better than the Z7 II?

Both use essentially the same 45.7 MP BSI sensor. The Z7 II adds IBIS (5-axis, up to 5 stops), better video, and mirrorless adaptability with native Z lenses. The D850 wins on optical viewfinder clarity, battery life (1,840 shots vs 420 CIPA), and F-mount native lens compatibility. For photographers with an F-mount lens collection, the D850 remains a compelling alternative to migrating to Z.

Does the D850 have an electronic shutter?

No. The Nikon D850 uses a conventional focal-plane mechanical shutter for all shooting. Every frame increments the mechanical counter. This makes the shutter count a reliable and direct indicator of total usage, unlike cameras with electronic shutter modes.

Can the Nikon D850 shutter count be reset?

Only Nikon authorised service centres can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original NEF file, not a screenshot or JPEG.
