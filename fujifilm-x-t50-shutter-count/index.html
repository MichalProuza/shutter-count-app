Fujifilm X-T50 — Shutter Rating

The Fujifilm X-T50 is Fujifilm's 2024 compact mid-range APS-C mirrorless camera. It packs the same 40.2 MP X-Trans CMOS 5 HR sensor as the X-T5 into a smaller, lighter body with a dedicated film simulation dial on top. Aimed at street and travel photographers who want maximum resolution in a pocketable form, its 150,000-actuation shutter rating is appropriate for enthusiast use.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Fujifilm X-T50

  1. Take a still photo with your Fujifilm X-T50 using the mechanical shutter and locate the .RAF file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the RAF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count and remaining life percentage appear instantly. No upload, no account required.
Counter reset warning: RAF MakerNote tag 0x1438 may reset to zero after formatting the memory card. This is a known Fujifilm limitation. Always verify from a recent RAF file when evaluating a used X-T50.

Fujifilm X-T50 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Fujifilm X-T50?

Shoot a mechanical RAF frame, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from tag 0x1438 in your browser.

What is the rated shutter life of the Fujifilm X-T50?

Fujifilm rates the X-T50 at 150,000 actuations — half the X-T5 (300,000) but adequate for enthusiast and travel photography.

Should I buy an X-T50 or X-T5?

The X-T5 offers double the shutter life (300,000), weather sealing, and a larger grip. The X-T50 is smaller, lighter, and features the dedicated film simulation dial. For high-volume shooting, choose the X-T5. For casual and travel use, the X-T50 is excellent.

Can the X-T50 shutter count be reset?

The RAF image counter may reset after card formatting. The hardware counter can only be reset by a Fujifilm authorised service centre after shutter replacement.
