How Does ShutterCount Read the A6400 Shutter Count?

The Sony A6400 stores the shutter count encrypted in ARW MakerNote tag 0x9050. ShutterCount applies Sony's cubic residue decryption (modulo 249) and reads the count from byte offset 0x003a — the same offset used by the A6100, A6300, A6500, and A6600.

All processing happens locally in your browser. The ARW file is never transmitted anywhere.

Sony A6400 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Sony A6400?

Shoot an ARW file, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is decrypted from tag 0x9050 at offset 0x003a in your browser.

What is the rated shutter life of the Sony A6400?

Sony rates the A6400 at 200,000 actuations — the same as the A6600 and the newer A6700.

Should I buy an A6400 or A6700?

The A6700 brings a stacked sensor, AI AF, and improved video. But a used A6400 with a low shutter count costs a fraction of a new A6700 and still delivers excellent photos with real-time Eye AF. For stills-focused work on a budget, the A6400 remains an outstanding choice.

Can the Sony A6400 shutter count be reset or faked?

Only a Sony authorised service centre can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original ARW file, not a screenshot.
