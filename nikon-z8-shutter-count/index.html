The Nikon Z8 is Nikon's 2023 professional full-frame mirrorless camera. It shares the 45.7 MP stacked BSI CMOS sensor of the flagship Z9, but packages it in a more compact body with a conventional mechanical shutter — giving it a key advantage over the Z9 for photographers who want a verifiable actuation count.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Nikon Z8

The Nikon Z8 embeds the shutter count in MakerNote tag 0x00A7 of every NEF file shot with the mechanical shutter or EFCS.

  1. Take any photo with your Nikon Z8 using the mechanical shutter (not silent/electronic mode) and locate the .NEF file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the NEF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count and remaining life percentage appear instantly. No upload, no account required.

If you shot the frame in electronic shutter mode, the count in tag 0x00A7 will reflect the last mechanical exposure count, not the electronic ones. Use a frame shot in mechanical mode for the most current reading.

How Does ShutterCount Read the Z8 Shutter Count?

Nikon's NEF format is a standard TIFF/EP container. ShutterNote locates the Nikon MakerNote IFD and reads tag 0x00A7 (ShutterCount). This tag is universally supported across all Nikon bodies from the D40 through to the Z8 — no model-specific offset tables required.

Does the Z8's electronic shutter mode count?

No. When shooting in electronic (silent) shutter mode, the mechanical curtain does not fire and tag 0x00A7 is not incremented. Only mechanical shutter and EFCS exposures are counted. If you primarily use electronic mode, your mechanical count is lower than your total frame count.

Can I use a JPEG to check the Z8 count?

NEF files are recommended for guaranteed accuracy. Some Nikon bodies embed the count in JPEG EXIF as well — if you only have a JPEG, try it in ShutterCount and it will indicate whether the count was found.

Nikon Z8 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Nikon Z8?

Shoot a NEF file with the mechanical shutter, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser — no upload needed.

What is the rated shutter life of the Nikon Z8?

Nikon rates the Z8 at 500,000 actuations, matching the Z9's sensor but with the addition of a mechanical shutter that makes the count verifiable.

Why doesn't the Nikon Z9 have a readable shutter count?

The Z9 has no mechanical shutter — it uses only the stacked sensor's electronic readout. Because the mechanical curtain never fires, tag 0x00A7 in the Z9's NEF files remains at zero regardless of how many frames have been shot. The Z8 was specifically designed to retain a mechanical shutter for this and other reasons.

Can the shutter count on a Nikon Z8 be reset or faked?

Only a Nikon authorised service centre can reset the hardware counter after a physical shutter replacement. Always verify from an original NEF file, not a screenshot.
