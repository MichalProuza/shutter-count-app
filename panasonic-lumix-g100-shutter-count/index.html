Panasonic Lumix G100 — Shutter Ratings

The Lumix G100 is Panasonic's compact, vlog-oriented Micro Four Thirds camera, launched in 2020. It features a 20.3 MP Dual Native ISO sensor, 3-axis IBIS, built-in directional 3-mic audio, and 4K/30p video. Like all Lumix cameras, its shutter count is not accessible from RW2 RAW files.

Model Release Sensor Est. Shutter Life RAW Format
Panasonic Lumix G100 (DC-G100) 2020 20.3 MP MFT ~200,000 RW2
Panasonic Lumix G110 (DC-G110) 2020 20.3 MP MFT ~200,000 RW2
Note: Panasonic does not publish official shutter ratings for the G100. The ~200,000 figure is an estimate based on comparable G-series bodies. The G100 is primarily a vlog/video camera — many used bodies have low shutter counts from heavy video use, since video recording uses the electronic shutter and adds zero mechanical actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Panasonic Lumix G100

RW2 files do not contain shutter count data. Unlike Canon CR3 or Nikon NEF files, Panasonic RW2 files do not embed the shutter counter in the MakerNote. You must check via the camera itself.

Method 1: Camera Menu

  1. Power on your Lumix G100 and press the MENU button.
  2. Navigate to the Setup Menu (wrench icon).
  3. Scroll to Camera Information Display or Version Display and select it.
  4. The shutter count is displayed alongside firmware version information.

Method 2: Lumix Sync App

  1. Install the Lumix Sync app on your smartphone.
  2. Connect the G100 to your phone via Wi-Fi (use the camera's Wi-Fi menu).
  3. In the app, navigate to camera information — the shutter count is displayed there.

Note: ShutterCount.app can parse the RW2 file header and display EXIF metadata, but the shutter count itself will not be present since Panasonic does not include it in the file.

What Is a Good Shutter Count for a Used Lumix G100?

The G100 is primarily a vlog and video camera. Many used bodies have accumulated most of their use in video mode (electronic shutter), keeping the mechanical shutter count very low. This is normal and not a concern. Only stills shot with the mechanical shutter increment the counter.

Actuation Count % of Est. Life Assessment
0 – 20,000 0 – 10 % Very low use — near new
20,000 – 60,000 10 – 30 % Low use — plenty of life remaining
60,000 – 120,000 30 – 60 % Moderate use — normal
120,000 – 170,000 60 – 85 % High use — negotiate price
170,000 + 85 %+ Near est. limit — factor in shutter replacement (~€100–200)
Buying tip: A G100 with high video hours but a low mechanical shutter count is typically a good buy. Video recording uses electronic shutter and does not wear the mechanical shutter at all. Ask the seller how the camera was primarily used.

Panasonic Lumix G100 — FAQ

Does 4K Photo mode on the G100 count toward shutter actuations?

No. 4K Photo mode captures frames using the electronic shutter at high speed. No mechanical shutter movement occurs, so the counter is not incremented. This is one of the reasons why high-use G100 bodies may still have very low mechanical shutter counts.

Is the G100 the same as the G110?

Yes. The Lumix G100 (DC-G100) and Lumix G110 (DC-G110) are the same camera sold under different names in different regions. The G110 name is used primarily in Europe. Both are identical in terms of shutter mechanism, RAW file behavior, and shutter count checking methods.

Can I check the shutter count from a JPEG?

No. Like RW2, JPEG files from Lumix cameras do not contain the shutter count in their MakerNote metadata. The only reliable methods are the camera menu and the Lumix Sync app.
