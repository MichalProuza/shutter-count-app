Canon EOS R10 — Shutter Rating

The Canon EOS R10 is Canon's 2022 entry-to-mid APS-C mirrorless camera, featuring a 24.2 MP sensor with Dual Pixel CMOS AF II, 23 fps electronic shutter burst, and 4K video. It occupies the step above the R50 in Canon's RF-S APS-C lineup, with a slightly faster burst rate and better ergonomics while maintaining the same 100,000-actuation shutter rating.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Canon EOS R10

Canon EOS R10 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Canon EOS R10?

Shoot a CR3 RAW file, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from the MakerNote in your browser.

What is the rated shutter life of the Canon EOS R10?

Canon rates the EOS R10 at 100,000 actuations — the same as the R50.

Is 70,000 actuations a lot for a Canon EOS R10?

Yes — at 70,000 on a 100,000-rated body, the R10 has used 70% of its rated shutter life. The shutter is likely to need replacement within the next 20,000–40,000 frames. Factor this cost into your buying decision.

Can the Canon R10 shutter count be reset?

Only a Canon authorised service centre can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original CR3 file.
