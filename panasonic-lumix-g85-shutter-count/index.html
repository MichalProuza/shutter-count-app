Panasonic Lumix G85 — Shutter Rating

The Panasonic Lumix G85 (2016) brought professional-grade features — weather sealing, 5-axis Dual IS 2, and 4K video — to a compact Micro Four Thirds body at an accessible price. Its 16 MP sensor (no low-pass filter) is paired with a mechanical focal-plane shutter rated at 200,000 actuations. The camera is known in Europe and Asia as the Lumix G80.

Key shooting features include 4K/30p video, 4K Photo mode (burst extraction from 4K frames), 5-axis Dual IS 2 (sensor + lens cooperation), and a fully articulating touchscreen. The absence of a low-pass filter improves fine detail resolution at the cost of a very slight moiré risk on certain patterns.

Shutter count not in RW2 files: Panasonic does not embed the shutter count in RW2 RAW files. Dropping a G85 RW2 file into shuttercount.app will return EXIF metadata (camera model, settings, date) but not the actuation count. Use the camera menu or Lumix Sync app instead.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Panasonic Lumix G85

  1. Via camera menu: Go to MENU → Setup (wrench icon) → Body Battery Info. The shutter actuation count is displayed on this screen.
  2. Via ExifTool: Run exiftool -ShutterCount yourfile.RW2 in a terminal. Some G85 firmware versions embed a shot count in Panasonic-specific MakerNote tags, though this is inconsistent and not officially documented.
  3. Via Lumix Sync app: Connect the G85 via Wi-Fi to the Lumix Sync smartphone app. Device information including shutter count can be accessed through the app's remote control interface.
  4. When buying used, request a live demo with the camera powered on showing the menu screen, or ask for a screenshot of the Body Battery Info page. Sellers who claim the count is inaccessible should be treated with caution.

What Is a Good Shutter Count for a Used Panasonic Lumix G85?

The G85 was released in 2016 and has been popular among travel and street photographers. Used bodies vary widely by shooting style — stills-heavy users accumulate higher counts than those who primarily shoot video (which uses the electronic shutter).

4K Photo and video use: The G85's 4K Photo mode (extracting stills from 4K video bursts) and all video recording modes use the electronic shutter — they do not increment the mechanical counter. A G85 used heavily for 4K Photo or video may have an unexpectedly low mechanical shutter count, which is normal and not a red flag.

Panasonic Lumix G85 — FAQ

What is the rated shutter life of the Panasonic Lumix G85?

The G85 mechanical shutter is rated at 200,000 actuations — the same as the GH5, GH6, and G9.

Is the Panasonic G85 still worth buying in 2026?

For budget-conscious photographers wanting weather sealing, excellent IBIS, and 4K video in a compact Micro Four Thirds body, the G85 remains competitive. Its 16 MP sensor provides adequate resolution for most uses, though the 20 MP sensors in the G9 and GH5 offer more detail. Prices for used G85 bodies have dropped significantly, making it exceptional value.

Does video recording wear the G85 shutter?

No. All video modes on the G85 use the sensor's electronic rolling shutter readout. The mechanical focal-plane shutter only fires when taking still photos in mechanical shutter mode. 4K Photo mode, which extracts JPEG frames from a 4K video buffer, also uses the electronic shutter and does not add mechanical wear.

What should I inspect when buying a used G85?

Beyond shutter count: check the weather seals around the battery door, SD card slot, and lens mount for cracking or compression (older rubber can harden and fail); verify IBIS operation with a slow-shutter test shot; inspect the articulating LCD hinge for looseness; and check 4K video recording on both the internal card and via HDMI.
