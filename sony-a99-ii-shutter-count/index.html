Sony Alpha 99 II — About the Shutter

The Sony Alpha 99 II (2016), model number ILCA-99M2, is Sony's last and most capable A-mount full-frame flagship. Combining a 42.4 MP BSI-CMOS sensor with Sony's SLT (Single Lens Translucent) fixed-mirror technology, it delivers 12 fps continuous shooting with continuous AF — a combination impossible on traditional DSLRs of the same era. It inherited the A-mount lens ecosystem covering hundreds of native lenses and generations of Minolta AF glass.

SLT technology and shutter count: unlike a DSLR mirror that flips up before each shot, the a99 II's translucent mirror is fixed. Every shot directly actuates the mechanical shutter — which is why Sony engineers the shutter for a high 500,000-actuation life on this body. A high count on a used a99 II is far less alarming than it would be on an entry-level camera.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Sony Alpha 99 II

The Sony a99 II does not expose the shutter count in its menu system. The counter is stored in the encrypted MakerNote tag 0x9050 within every ARW RAW file.

  1. Set your a99 II to shoot RAW or RAW + JPEG and take at least one photo.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge).
  3. Drag the resulting .ARW file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count is shown immediately. No upload, no account, no plugins needed.

Processing runs entirely on your device. The ARW file is never sent to any server, making it safe to check cameras you are evaluating for purchase.

Can I use a JPEG from my a99 II?

Sony frequently strips the MakerNote from in-camera JPEGs, removing the shutter count. For reliable results, always use an ARW RAW file. Enable RAW shooting in the Quality/Image Size menu before taking the test shot.

Sony a99 II Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Sony Alpha 99 II?

Shoot an ARW RAW file, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is extracted from the encrypted MakerNote tag entirely in your browser — no upload needed.

What is the Sony a99 II officially rated at?

Sony officially rates the ILCA-99M2 shutter at 500,000 actuations — one of the highest published ratings of any Sony camera and significantly higher than entry or mid-range bodies.

Does SLT technology wear the shutter faster?

No — the fixed translucent mirror never moves, so it contributes zero wear. Only the focal-plane shutter curtains increment the counter. Sony designs the shutter specifically for high-cycle professional use, reflected in the 500,000-actuation rating.

Can the shutter count be reset or faked?

Only a Sony authorised service centre can reset the counter following a physical shutter replacement. EXIF-editing software can modify the file's metadata but cannot alter the in-camera counter. Always verify from an original, unmodified ARW file.
