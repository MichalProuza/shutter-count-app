Sony FX3 at a Glance

The Sony FX3, released in March 2021, is Sony's most compact Cinema Line camera. Built on the same 12.1 MP full-frame sensor as the A7S III, it adds active cooling for unlimited recording, a built-in XLR handle, tally lamp, and a cinema-oriented body design. It quickly became a favorite for solo filmmakers and run-and-gun productions.

The FX3 shutter is rated at 200,000 actuations. However, since this is primarily a video camera, shutter count alone tells only part of the story when evaluating a used FX3.

How to Check Sony FX3 Shutter Count

  1. Switch your FX3 to still image mode (photo mode).
  2. Take a fresh photo of any subject.
  3. Transfer the ARW file to your computer via USB or SD card reader.
  4. Go to shuttercount.app and upload the ARW file.
  5. The shutter count appears within seconds — no account or installation needed.
Technical note: Sony embeds shutter actuations in an encrypted MakerNote block (EXIF tag 0x9050). For the FX3, the count is stored at offset 0x003a using a cubic residue cipher modulo 249. Shuttercount.app decrypts and reads this automatically.

Does Video Recording Affect the Count?

No. Video recording on the Sony FX3 does not increment the shutter counter in the ARW MakerNote. The counter only reflects still photo actuations. Since the FX3 is primarily a cinema camera, most usage is video — meaning a heavily-used FX3 can show a very low shutter count.

Buyer warning: A low shutter count on an FX3 does not mean light use. Most FX3 owners shoot primarily video, which leaves no trace in the shutter counter. Always ask about total recording hours, check the fan and cooling system, and look for signs of overheating or physical wear when buying used.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I check the shutter count on a Sony FX3?

Switch to still photo mode, take a photo, upload the ARW file to shuttercount.app, and the tool reads the count from the encrypted MakerNote at offset 0x003a. The result is instant and requires no software installation.

What is the Sony FX3 shutter life rating?

Sony rates the FX3 at 200,000 actuations. Since the FX3 is primarily used for video, most bodies will never approach this rating through stills alone.

Does video recording affect the Sony FX3 shutter count?

No. Video recording does not increment the mechanical shutter counter. Only still photo actuations are counted in the ARW MakerNote.

Is the Sony FX3 shutter count meaningful for a cinema camera?

Less so than for a stills camera. Since most FX3 usage is video (which isn't counted), a low shutter count doesn't necessarily mean light use. Evaluate total recording hours, cooling system condition, and physical wear alongside the count.

What is the difference between Sony FX3 and A7S III for shutter count?

Both share the same 12.1 MP sensor and use MakerNote offset 0x003a. Shutter count checking is identical for both models on shuttercount.app. The FX3 adds active cooling, XLR audio, and a cinema body design.
