Panasonic Lumix GH6 — Shutter Rating

The Panasonic Lumix GH6 (2022) is the flagship Micro Four Thirds camera in Panasonic's GH video line. It features a newly developed 25.2 MP MOS sensor, Dual Native ISO, unlimited 4K recording, Apple ProRes internal recording (a first for the GH line), and support for 5.7K/60p and C4K/120p in 10-bit. The mechanical shutter is rated at 200,000 actuations.

Shutter count not in RW2 files: Panasonic does not embed the shutter count in RW2 RAW files. Dropping a GH6 RW2 file into shuttercount.app will display EXIF metadata (camera model, settings, date) but not the actuation count. Use the camera menu or Lumix Sync app to retrieve the count.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Panasonic Lumix GH6

  1. Via camera menu: Go to MENU → Setup (wrench icon) → Body Battery Info. The total shutter count is typically displayed here.
  2. Via Lumix Sync app: Connect the GH6 via USB or Wi-Fi to the Panasonic Lumix Sync app on your smartphone. Navigate to device information to view the total shot count.
  3. Via ExifTool: Run exiftool -ShutterCount yourfile.RW2 — this reads Panasonic MakerNote tags and may return a shot count on some firmware versions, though it is not guaranteed.
  4. When buying used, always ask the seller for a screenshot of the camera menu showing the shutter count.

Panasonic Lumix GH6 — FAQ

What is the rated shutter life of the Panasonic Lumix GH6?

The GH6 mechanical shutter is rated at 200,000 actuations. This is the same rating as the GH5 and consistent with other professional Micro Four Thirds bodies.

Does video recording wear out the shutter?

No. Video recording uses the sensor's electronic rolling shutter readout, not the mechanical focal-plane shutter. Only still photos captured in mechanical shutter mode increment the actuation counter. This is an important distinction for used GH6 buyers, since many of these cameras have been used heavily for video production.

How does the GH6 compare to the GH5 for shutter count purposes?

Both the GH5 and GH6 are rated at 200,000 mechanical shutter actuations. The GH6's key advantages are its higher-resolution 25.2 MP sensor, Apple ProRes recording, and significantly improved video capabilities. The shutter mechanism and RW2 file behavior are essentially the same — neither stores the count in RAW files.

Is the GH6 a good used camera to buy?

The GH6 is an excellent used buy for video-focused users. Because much of its use is video (which doesn't wear the mechanical shutter), used bodies often have far lower mechanical shutter counts than their total usage would suggest. Focus on firmware version, sensor condition, and heat-related issues from long recording sessions when evaluating a used GH6.
