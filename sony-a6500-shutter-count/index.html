Sony A6500 at a Glance

The Sony A6500, released in November 2016, was Sony's first APS-C mirrorless camera with in-body image stabilization (IBIS). Packing a 24.2 MP Exmor CMOS sensor, 425 phase-detection AF points, and 11 fps continuous shooting, it became the go-to choice for action photographers and videographers who needed a compact, capable body.

The A6500 shutter is rated at 200,000 actuations — the same as its successor, the A6600. Understanding the shutter count is essential when buying or selling a used A6500, especially given the camera's age in 2026.

How to Check Sony A6500 Shutter Count

  1. Set your A6500 to single-shot mechanical shutter mode (not silent/electronic).
  2. Take a fresh photo of any subject — indoors is fine.
  3. Transfer the ARW file to your computer via USB or SD card reader.
  4. Go to shuttercount.app and upload the ARW file.
  5. The shutter count appears within seconds — no account or installation needed.
Technical note: Sony embeds shutter actuations in an encrypted MakerNote block (EXIF tag 0x9050). For the A6500, the count is stored at offset 0x003a using a cubic residue cipher modulo 249. Shuttercount.app decrypts and reads this automatically.

Does Electronic Shutter Affect the Count?

No. The Sony A6500 supports silent electronic shutter shooting, but these exposures do not increment the mechanical shutter counter in the ARW MakerNote. Only physical mechanical shutter actuations are counted.

Buyer warning: If a seller has used the A6500 extensively in silent mode (e.g., for indoor events or video work), the mechanical count will appear lower than the total camera use. Always ask about usage patterns alongside the shutter count figure.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I check the shutter count on a Sony A6500?

Take a mechanical shutter photo, upload the ARW file to shuttercount.app, and the tool reads the count from the encrypted MakerNote at offset 0x003a. The result is instant and requires no software installation.

What is the Sony A6500 shutter life rating?

Sony rates the A6500 shutter at 200,000 actuations. This matches the A6600 and A7 III, placing it in the mid-tier durability range for Sony mirrorless cameras.

Does the Sony A6500 shutter count include electronic shutter shots?

No. Only mechanical shutter actuations are counted. Silent electronic shutter shots do not increment the counter in the ARW MakerNote.

Is 50,000 shutter count high for a used Sony A6500?

No — 50,000 is 25% of the 200,000 rating. This indicates light to moderate use and is a good buy at the right price. Counts above 150,000 warrant more scrutiny.

What is the difference between Sony A6500 and A6600 for shutter count checking?

Both models use the same MakerNote offset 0x003a in the encrypted tag 0x9050 block. Same shutter rating (200,000), same encoding. Shuttercount.app handles both automatically.
