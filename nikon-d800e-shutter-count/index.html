Nikon D800E — Shutter Rating & Overview

The Nikon D800E (2012) is a variant of the groundbreaking D800, featuring a modified optical low-pass filter that cancels the anti-aliasing effect for maximum resolving power from its 36.3 MP full-frame CMOS sensor. Paired with Nikon’s 51-point Multi-CAM 3500FX autofocus, weather-sealed magnesium body, dual card slots (CF + SD), and 4 fps burst, it became the sharpest DSLR of its era — a favourite among landscape, studio, and fine-art photographers. The mechanical shutter is rated at 200,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Nikon D800E

  1. Take a still photo with your Nikon D800E and locate the .NEF file on the CF or SD card.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the NEF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The mechanical shutter count appears instantly, read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser.

The D800E uses a mechanical shutter for all exposures — every photo increments the counter.

How Does ShutterCount Read the Nikon D800E Shutter Count?

The Nikon D800E stores the mechanical shutter count in NEF MakerNote EXIF tag 0x00A7 (decimal 167). This is the universal Nikon shutter count field, used identically across all Nikon DSLRs and Z-series mirrorless cameras. Nikon D800E Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Nikon D800E?

Shoot a NEF frame and drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser, instantly and privately.

What is the rated shutter life of the Nikon D800E?

Nikon rates the D800E at 200,000 actuations. This is the same professional-grade rating as the standard D800 and the successor D810.

What is the difference between the D800 and D800E?

The D800E has a modified optical low-pass filter that cancels the anti-aliasing effect, delivering maximum sharpness from the 36.3 MP sensor. The standard D800 includes a traditional AA filter that slightly softens the image to prevent moiré patterns. The sensor, autofocus, shutter, body, and all other specs are identical.

Is the D800E still worth buying?

For landscape, architecture, and studio work, the D800E remains remarkably capable. Its 36.3 MP sensor without AA filter delivers outstanding detail that rivals many newer cameras. The main limitations are slower autofocus tracking and older video capabilities compared to modern bodies. At today’s used prices, it represents exceptional value per megapixel.

Can the Nikon D800E shutter count be reset?

Only Nikon authorised service centres can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original NEF file, not a screenshot or JPEG.
