Fujifilm X-E3 — Compact X-Trans III Rangefinder

The Fujifilm X-E3 (2017) is the compact rangefinder-style sibling to the X-T2 and X-Pro2. Featuring the same 24.3 MP X-Trans CMOS III sensor and X-Processor Pro, it brought flagship-class image quality to a much smaller and lighter body. The X-E3 also introduced touchscreen gestures, a focus joystick, and Bluetooth connectivity to the X-E line. Its understated design makes it a favourite for street photographers who want top image quality without drawing attention.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Fujifilm X-E3

The X-E3 does not display the shutter count in its menus. The count is embedded in every RAF RAW file.

  1. Take any photo with your Fujifilm X-E3 and locate the .RAF file on the SD card.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the RAF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count appears instantly. No upload, no account, no plugins required.

How Does ShutterCount Read the X-E3 Shutter Count?

The Fujifilm X-E3 writes images in Fujifilm's RAF format. ShutterCount parses the RAF container, locates the embedded EXIF/MakerNote structure, and reads the shutter count from the Fujifilm-specific metadata block. All processing happens in your browser — the file never leaves your device.

Does the electronic shutter affect the count?

No. The X-E3's electronic shutter bypasses the mechanical curtain entirely and does not increment the shutter count. Only mechanical shutter exposures are counted.

Fujifilm X-E3 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Fujifilm X-E3?

Shoot a RAF file and drop it into shuttercount.app. The shutter count is read from the Fujifilm MakerNote in your browser — no upload needed.

What is the rated shutter life of the X-E3?

The X-E3 is rated for approximately 150,000 actuations. This is a conservative estimate; many units exceed it in real-world use.

Is the X-E3 still worth buying in 2026?

Yes. The 24 MP X-Trans III sensor still produces excellent stills with Fujifilm's signature colour rendering, and the compact rangefinder body is ideal for street and travel work. Used prices have remained reasonable, making it a sensible alternative to the more expensive X-E4.

What is the difference between the X-E3 and X-E4?

The X-E4 (2021) upgraded to a 26 MP X-Trans IV sensor, added a tilting touchscreen, and switched to a more minimalist control layout (no front grip, no rear D-pad). The X-E3 retains the more conventional dial layout that some shooters still prefer.

Can the X-E3 shutter count be reset?

Only Fujifilm authorised service centres can reset the hardware counter after a shutter replacement. Always verify from an original RAF file.
