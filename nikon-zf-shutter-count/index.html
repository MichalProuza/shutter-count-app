Nikon Zf — Shutter Rating

The Nikon Zf is Nikon's 2023 retro-inspired full-frame mirrorless camera. Modelled on the aesthetic of the classic Nikon FM2 film camera, it features physical dedicated dials for shutter speed, ISO, and exposure compensation alongside a 24.5 MP BSI CMOS sensor and in-body image stabilisation. It is aimed at photographers who value a tactile, analogue-feeling shooting experience with modern full-frame capability. Its 200,000-actuation shutter rating is standard for this class of body.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Nikon Zf

  1. Take any photo with your Nikon Zf and locate the .NEF file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the NEF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count and remaining life percentage appear instantly. No upload, no account required.

How Does ShutterCount Read the Nikon Zf Shutter Count?

The Nikon Zf stores the shutter count in MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in every NEF file — identical to all Nikon Z-series and DSLR bodies. ShutterCount reads this directly from the NEF structure with no model-specific configuration needed.

Nikon Zf Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Nikon Zf?

Shoot a NEF file, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from tag 0x00A7 in your browser.

What is the rated shutter life of the Nikon Zf?

Nikon rates the Zf at 200,000 actuations.

Is the Nikon Zf or Z5 II better for long-term use?

Both are rated at 200,000 actuations. The Zf offers more physical controls and IBIS; the Z5 II is more traditional in form with slightly more conventional ergonomics. For very high-volume shooting, both are outclassed by the Z6 III (400,000 rated).

Can the Nikon Zf shutter count be reset?

Only a Nikon authorised service centre can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original NEF file.
