Panasonic Lumix GH7 — Shutter Rating

The Panasonic Lumix GH7 (2024) is the flagship of the GH series and the most capable Micro Four Thirds video camera at the time of its release. It introduces a new 25.2 MP sensor with a phase-detection AF system — a first for the GH line, which previously relied on Panasonic's DFD contrast-detection system. The GH7 supports internal C4K/60p 10-bit, ProRes recording via CFexpress, and Apple ProRes RAW output over HDMI. Its mechanical shutter is rated at 200,000 actuations.

The camera is aimed primarily at video professionals and serious hybrid shooters. Its mechanical shutter is used mainly for stills; video always uses the electronic rolling shutter readout.

Shutter count not in RW2 files: Panasonic does not embed the shutter count in RW2 RAW files. Dropping a GH7 RW2 file into shuttercount.app will return EXIF metadata (model, exposure settings, date) but not the actuation count. Check via the camera menu or Lumix Sync app.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Panasonic Lumix GH7

  1. Via camera menu: Press MENU → Setup (wrench) → Body Battery Info. The shutter actuation count is shown here alongside battery charge cycles.
  2. Via Lumix Sync app: Connect the GH7 to the Lumix Sync smartphone app via Wi-Fi or USB. The device information screen includes the total shutter count.
  3. Via ExifTool: Run exiftool -ShutterCount yourfile.RW2. Some Panasonic firmware versions embed a shot count in MakerNote tags, though this is not officially documented and not guaranteed for all GH7 firmware revisions.
  4. When buying a used GH7, request a live demonstration of the camera menu shutter count screen, or ask for a screenshot. Given the GH7's 2024 release, used units should have low counts.

What Is a Good Shutter Count for a Used Panasonic Lumix GH7?

The GH7 was released in 2024, so most used examples on the secondary market will have low counts. A GH7 used heavily for video may have a very low mechanical shutter count despite significant overall use — this is expected and not a concern.

Panasonic Lumix GH7 — FAQ

What is the rated shutter life of the Panasonic Lumix GH7?

The GH7 mechanical shutter is rated at 200,000 actuations — the same as the GH5, GH6, and GH5S.

What makes the GH7 different from the GH6?

The GH7 adds phase-detection autofocus (the GH6 uses contrast-only DFD AF), internal ProRes recording on CFexpress Type B cards, Apple ProRes RAW HDMI output, a slightly improved processor, and enhanced subject recognition AI. The sensor resolution (25.2 MP) and shutter rating (200,000) are unchanged from the GH6.

Does the GH7 support internal RAW video?

The GH7 can record internal ProRes (not RAW) on a CFexpress Type B card. Apple ProRes RAW (actual raw sensor data) requires an external recorder connected via HDMI. Internal recording uses processed video data regardless of the codec.

What should I check when buying a used GH7?

Verify: shutter count via menu, CFexpress card slot functionality (test with a compatible card), HDMI port integrity (used by many videographers for monitoring or ProRes RAW output), weather sealing condition (check rubber door gaskets), and that the fan ventilation is unobstructed and operating correctly.
