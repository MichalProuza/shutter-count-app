Panasonic Lumix S1H — Shutter Rating & Cinema Context

The Panasonic Lumix S1H (2019) was the world's first full-frame mirrorless camera to receive Netflix certification for original content production. Built on the same 24.2 MP Dual Native ISO full-frame sensor as the S1, it adds unlimited 6K/24p and 4K/60p recording, a fully articulating rear LCD, a large heat-dissipating body, and V-Log pre-installed. The mechanical shutter is rated at approximately 200,000 actuations, but the S1H is fundamentally a video tool — many units accumulate hundreds of hours of video recording while keeping mechanical shutter counts extremely low.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Panasonic Lumix S1H

  1. Power on the camera and press the MENU button.
  2. Navigate to the Setup menu (wrench icon, last tab).
  3. Scroll to the last pages of the setup menu and look for Shutter Count.
  4. The total mechanical shutter actuation count is displayed on screen.

Alternative: Connect to the Lumix Sync smartphone app. The app displays the shutter count on the camera info screen when paired via Wi-Fi.

Note: Video recording does not increment the mechanical shutter counter. If a seller claims low use based on shutter count alone, also ask about total video recording hours and inspect for signs of professional operation (heat damage, worn grip, replaced screens).

Why Don't RW2 Files Store the Shutter Count?

Panasonic chose not to embed the shutter count in the RW2 (Raw Version 2) file format, unlike Fujifilm (RAF) or Nikon (NEF). The count is stored in the camera's internal firmware memory and is only accessible via the camera menu or app. This means the shuttercount.app browser tool cannot read the S1H shutter count from a RW2 file — you must use the camera menu method described above.

Panasonic Lumix S1H Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Panasonic Lumix S1H?

Go to MENU → Setup (wrench) → Shutter Count. Alternatively, use the Lumix Sync app. RW2 files do not embed the count, so file-based tools cannot read it.

What is the rated shutter life of the Panasonic Lumix S1H?

The S1H mechanical shutter is rated at approximately 200,000 actuations. Note the S1 (stills-focused sibling) has a higher 400,000-actuation rating.

Is low shutter count a reliable indicator of condition for the S1H?

Not entirely. The S1H is a cinema camera. A unit may have thousands of hours of video recording with only a few hundred still actuations. Evaluate total video use, heat management (check for thermal compound dryness if accessible), body cosmetics, and lens mount wear as well.

S1H vs S1 — what are the key differences?

The S1H adds Netflix certification, unlimited video recording at 6K and 4K/60p, an additional cooling fan, fully articulating LCD (vs tilting on S1), and V-Log pre-installed. The S1 has a higher 400,000-actuation shutter rating and is better balanced for stills. Both share the same 24.2 MP sensor and L-mount.

Can I use shuttercount.app to read the S1H shutter count?

No. Panasonic does not embed the shutter count in RW2 files. ShutterCount can read counts from Fujifilm RAF, Nikon NEF, Sony ARW, and Canon CR2/CR3 files. For Panasonic, use the camera menu method.
