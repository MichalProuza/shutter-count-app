Olympus PEN E-PL7 — Shutter Rating

The Olympus PEN E-PL7 (2014) is a stylish entry-level Micro Four Thirds mirrorless camera with a retro-inspired design. It features a 16 MP Live MOS sensor, 3-axis IBIS, a rear-facing 180° tilting touchscreen for selfies and vlogging, and built-in Wi-Fi. Unlike the OM-D series, the PEN line prioritises aesthetics and portability over advanced features. Olympus does not publish a rated shutter count for the E-PL7; community reports suggest approximately 100,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Olympus PEN E-PL7

  1. Via camera menu: Press MENU → Setup tab (spanner icon) → Camera Information. The shutter actuation count is displayed alongside firmware version details.
  2. Via ExifTool: Run exiftool -ShutterCount yourfile.ORF. ExifTool reads from Olympus MakerNote tags and may return a count, though consistency varies by firmware version.
  3. When buying used, ask the seller to photograph the Camera Information screen in the menu. This is easily accessible on-camera and requires no computer connection.

Olympus PEN E-PL7 — FAQ

What is the rated shutter life of the Olympus PEN E-PL7?

Olympus does not publish an official shutter rating for the PEN E-PL7. An estimate of approximately 100,000 actuations is widely cited, consistent with other entry-level Olympus PEN bodies.

Is the E-PL7 a good camera for beginners?

Yes. The E-PL7 offers solid image quality, an easy-to-use interface, compact size, and the broad Micro Four Thirds lens ecosystem. The 180° tilting touchscreen is convenient for vlogging and selfies. Key limitations: no electronic viewfinder, no 4K video (1080p maximum), and 3-axis IBIS rather than the 5-axis system in the OM-D series.

What Micro Four Thirds lenses work on the E-PL7?

All Micro Four Thirds lenses are compatible, including those from Olympus, OM System, Panasonic, Sigma, and third-party makers. This is a major advantage of the M4/3 standard — a lens purchased for the E-PL7 works natively on the E-M1 Mark III, OM System OM-1, or Panasonic G9 II.

Does the E-PL7 have weather sealing?

No. The E-PL7 is not splash or dustproof. Protect it from rain and dust. For weather-sealed Micro Four Thirds, consider the Olympus E-M5 series or the OM System OM-5.
