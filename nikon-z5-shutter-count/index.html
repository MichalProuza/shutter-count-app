Nikon Z5 — Shutter Rating

The Nikon Z5 is Nikon's 2020 entry-level full-frame mirrorless camera. Built around a 24.3 MP CMOS sensor (non-BSI), dual SD card slots, and 5-axis IBIS, the Z5 brings full-frame image quality at a budget price. Its 200,000-actuation shutter rating matches the Z6 II and is double the entry-level Z50 II (100,000).

How to Check Shutter Count on the Nikon Z5

  1. Take any photo with your Nikon Z5 and locate the .NEF file on your memory card or computer.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the NEF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count and remaining life percentage appear instantly. No upload, no account required.

How Does ShutterCount Read the Z5 Shutter Count?

Like all Nikon Z-series bodies, the Z5 stores the shutter count in MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in every NEF file. ShutterCount reads this tag universally across all Nikon bodies — no model-specific configuration required.

All processing happens locally in your browser. The NEF file is never transmitted anywhere.

Nikon Z5 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Nikon Z5?

Shoot a NEF RAW file, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser.

What is the rated shutter life of the Nikon Z5?

Nikon rates the Z5 at 200,000 actuations — identical to the Z6 II and sufficient for enthusiast to moderate professional use.

Should I buy a Z5 or wait for the Z5 II?

The Z5 II (2024) brings improved AF, a faster processor, and the same 200,000-actuation shutter rating. Used Z5 bodies offer excellent value if you prioritize image quality and dual card slots over cutting-edge AF performance.

Can the shutter count on a Nikon Z5 be reset or faked?

Only a Nikon authorised service centre can reset the hardware counter after a physical shutter replacement. Always verify from an original NEF file, not a screenshot.
