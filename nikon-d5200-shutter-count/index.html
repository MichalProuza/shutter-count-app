Nikon D5200 — Shutter Rating & Context

The Nikon D5200 (2012) brought 24.1 megapixels, a 39-point autofocus system, and an articulating LCD to Nikon’s mid-range DX lineup. It used a Toshiba-made sensor that delivered excellent dynamic range and detail, making it a favourite among enthusiast photographers. The mechanical shutter is rated at 100,000 actuations.

39-point AF upgrade: The D5200 moved from the 11-point AF system of the D5100 to a 39-point module (same as the D7000), significantly improving autofocus tracking for action and sports.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Nikon D5200

  1. Take a still photo with your Nikon D5200 and locate the .NEF file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the NEF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The mechanical shutter count appears instantly, read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser.

The D5200 does not have a silent or electronic shutter mode — every frame increments the mechanical shutter counter.

How Does ShutterCount Read the Nikon D5200 Shutter Count?

The Nikon D5200 stores the mechanical shutter count in NEF MakerNote EXIF tag 0x00A7 (decimal 167). This is the standard Nikon shutter count field. ShutterCount reads this tag directly in your browser — no encryption or decryption needed.

100% local: All processing happens in your browser. The NEF file never leaves your device.

Can I use a JPEG instead of a NEF file?

JPEGs from the D5200 do not contain the MakerNote data needed to read the shutter count. Always use the original .NEF raw file for an accurate reading.

Nikon D5200 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Nikon D5200?

Shoot a NEF frame and drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser, instantly and privately.

What is the rated shutter life of the Nikon D5200?

Nikon rates the D5200 at 100,000 actuations. Many units exceed this figure, but the failure risk increases after the rated limit.

How does the D5200 compare to the D5300?

The D5300 added built-in Wi-Fi and GPS, removed the optical low-pass filter for slightly sharper images, and improved the LCD resolution. Both share the same 24 MP class sensor and 39-point AF system, so image quality is very similar.

Can the Nikon D5200 shutter count be reset?

Only Nikon authorised service centres can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original NEF file, not a screenshot or JPEG.
