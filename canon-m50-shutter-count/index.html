Canon EOS M50 — Shutter Rating

The Canon EOS M50 (2018) was one of the best-selling mirrorless cameras of its generation. Built around a 24.1 MP APS-C CMOS sensor with Dual Pixel CMOS AF and Canon's DIGIC 8 processor, it became the go-to camera for vloggers and hobbyist photographers thanks to its compact body, flip-out touchscreen, and 4K video capability. It was also the first Canon mirrorless camera to use the CR3 RAW format. The mechanical shutter is rated at approximately 100,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Canon EOS M50

  1. Take any photo with your Canon EOS M50 and locate the .CR3 file on your memory card or computer.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the CR3 file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.

Canon EOS M50 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Canon EOS M50?

Shoot a CR3 RAW file and drop it into shuttercount.app. Support for this model is pending — the tool may not yet return a count for M50 CR3 files.

What is the rated shutter life of the Canon EOS M50?

Canon rates the EOS M50 at approximately 100,000 actuations. Most M50 owners use the camera primarily for video, so mechanical shutter wear is typically low.

Is the Canon EOS M50 still worth buying?

The M50 is still an excellent value on the used market for stills and casual video. Its EF-M mount has been discontinued by Canon in favour of the RF mount, but used EF-M lenses remain affordable. The Canon EOS R50 is the modern RF-mount successor.

Does the electronic shutter on the M50 affect the count?

No. Electronic shutter actuations bypass the mechanical curtain and are not counted. Only mechanical shutter actuations increment the counter.

Can the shutter count on a Canon EOS M50 be reset?

Only a Canon authorised service centre can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original CR3 file, not a screenshot or JPEG.
