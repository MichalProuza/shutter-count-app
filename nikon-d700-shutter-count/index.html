Nikon D700 — Shutter Rating & DSLR Context

The Nikon D700 (2008) was one of the most beloved professional DSLRs ever made. Built around a 12.1 MP FX-format CMOS sensor derived from the flagship D3, the D700 brought full-frame performance into a smaller, more affordable body. Its legendary low-light capability, rugged magnesium-alloy construction, and 8 fps burst rate (with MB-D10 grip) made it the workhorse of choice for photojournalists and wedding photographers worldwide. The mechanical shutter is rated at 150,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Nikon D700

  1. Take a still photo with your Nikon D700 and locate the .NEF file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the NEF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The mechanical shutter count appears instantly, read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser.

The D700 uses a conventional focal-plane mechanical shutter for all shooting — every frame increments the counter.

Nikon D700 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Nikon D700?

Shoot a NEF frame and drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser, instantly and privately.

What is the rated shutter life of the Nikon D700?

Nikon rates the D700 at 150,000 actuations. The shutter mechanism is the same dual-curtain unit used in the D300, designed for sustained professional use.

Is the Nikon D700 still worth buying?

For photographers who value build quality, low-light performance, and an optical viewfinder, the D700 remains outstanding. Its 12.1 MP sensor is low by modern standards but delivers excellent dynamic range and high-ISO performance. At current used prices, it is one of the most affordable ways to own a professional-grade Nikon body.

How does the D700 compare to the D750?

The D750 (2014) offers 24.3 MP resolution, better video, tilting screen, and improved AF. The D700 has a more robust body (magnesium alloy throughout, weather-sealed to a higher standard) and is compatible with the MB-D10 grip for 8 fps shooting. Both share the same 150,000-actuation shutter rating.

Can the Nikon D700 shutter count be reset?

Only Nikon authorised service centres can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original NEF file, not a screenshot or JPEG.
