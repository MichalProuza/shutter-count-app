Olympus OM-D E-M10 Mark III — Shutter Rating

The Olympus OM-D E-M10 Mark III (2017) is the third generation of the entry-level OM-D line. It features a 16.1 MP Micro Four Thirds Live MOS sensor, 5-axis in-body image stabilisation (4-stop rating), a 2.36M-dot EVF, a tilting touchscreen, and 4K/30p video. Designed for beginners and travel photographers, it offers the Olympus colour science and M4/3 lens ecosystem at an accessible price.

Olympus does not publish an official shutter rating for the E-M10 Mark III. The estimated lifespan is ~100,000 actuations, consistent with the E-M10 Mark IV and the camera's entry-level class positioning.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Olympus OM-D E-M10 Mark III

  1. Via camera INFO screen: With any image in playback mode, press the INFO button repeatedly to cycle through info displays. One view shows the camera's total shot count.
  2. Via ExifTool: Run exiftool -OlympusMakernote:ShutterCount yourfile.ORF. Some firmware versions embed the count in Olympus MakerNote, though it is not officially documented.
  3. When buying a used E-M10 Mark III, ask the seller to demonstrate the info screen live, or take a test shot and read the ORF in ExifTool yourself.
  4. Also inspect the shutter curtain and mirror area for dust and debris, sensor for hot pixels (shoot a plain surface at ISO 1600+ for 30 seconds), and the EVF for fungus.

Olympus OM-D E-M10 Mark III — FAQ

What is the estimated shutter life of the Olympus E-M10 Mark III?

Olympus does not publish an official rating. The estimated lifespan is ~100,000 actuations based on the camera's entry-level positioning, consistent with the E-M10 Mark II and Mark IV.

Does the E-M10 Mark III have weather sealing?

No. The E-M10 series has never been weather sealed. For a weather-sealed Micro Four Thirds option, consider the Olympus E-M5 Mark III, OM System OM-5, or the E-M1 series.

What M4/3 lenses work with the E-M10 Mark III?

All Micro Four Thirds lenses work on the E-M10 Mark III — including lenses from Olympus/OM System, Panasonic Lumix G, Sigma, Tamron, Laowa, and others. The M4/3 standard is maintained by both Olympus/OM System and Panasonic, ensuring a wide range of compatible optics.

Should I buy an E-M10 Mark III or Mark IV?

The Mark IV (2020) adds a 20.3 MP sensor (vs 16.1 MP), improved IBIS, and a selfie-flip LCD. If resolution matters, the Mark IV is the better choice. For budget-conscious buyers, the Mark III offers identical core functionality at a lower price with the same lens compatibility.
