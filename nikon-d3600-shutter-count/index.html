Nikon D3600 — Shutter Rating & Overview

The Nikon D3600 (2016) is Nikon's entry-level APS-C DSLR, positioned between the D3400 and the later D3500. It features a 24.2 MP APS-C CMOS sensor (no optical low-pass filter), Nikon's EXPEED 4 processor, and an articulated interface aimed at first-time DSLR buyers. The Guide Mode and SnapBridge Bluetooth connectivity made it a popular recommendation for beginners. The mechanical shutter is rated at 100,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Nikon D3600

The Nikon D3600 does not show the shutter count in its on-screen menus. The count is stored in the MakerNote section of every NEF RAW file the camera produces. ShutterCount reads it directly in your browser:

  1. Take any photo with your Nikon D3600 and locate the resulting .NEF file (do not use JPEGs — they may not contain the MakerNote with the shutter count).
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge).
  3. Drag the NEF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The exact shutter actuation count appears instantly. No upload, no account, no plugins required.

You can drop multiple NEF files at once to check several files in a single batch.

How Does ShutterCount Read the D3600 Shutter Count?

Nikon stores the shutter count in a proprietary MakerNote section embedded in the EXIF data of every NEF RAW file. Specifically, it reads tag 0x00A7 — a 4-byte unsigned integer representing the cumulative mechanical actuation count.

This tag is consistent across all Nikon DSLRs and Z-series mirrorless bodies, making it reliable and well-documented. ShutterCount parses the NEF file entirely in your browser using JavaScript — the file never leaves your device.

Why can't I use a JPEG from my D3600?

Nikon often omits or truncates the MakerNote in JPEG files exported from the D3600. For a guaranteed accurate count, always use the original .NEF RAW file. If you shoot RAW+JPEG, use the NEF.

Does Bluetooth transfer affect the count reading?

No. The SnapBridge Bluetooth connection on the D3600 transfers small copies of images for social sharing; it does not modify the MakerNote data. Always use the full-size original NEF from the memory card for the most accurate reading.

Nikon D3600 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Nikon D3600?

Take a photo, locate the NEF file on your memory card, and drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from MakerNote tag 0x00A7 in your browser — no upload, no account required.

What is the rated shutter life of the Nikon D3600?

Nikon rates the D3600 shutter at 100,000 actuations. This is the manufacturer-estimated median — individual shutters often last longer.

What is the difference between the D3600 and the D3500?

The D3500 (2018) replaced the D3600 with a redesigned, more comfortable grip and extended battery life (1,550 shots CIPA vs ~1,200 on the D3600). Both use the same 24.2 MP sensor, 100,000-actuation shutter, and NEF MakerNote tag 0x00A7. For shutter count purposes, they are identical.

Can the Nikon D3600 shutter count be reset or faked?

The internal hardware counter can only be reset by a Nikon authorised service centre after a physical shutter replacement. EXIF editing tools that rewrite metadata values do not affect the internal counter. Always verify from an original NEF RAW file — never from a screenshot.
