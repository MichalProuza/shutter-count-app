Leica M10-R — Shutter Rating

The Leica M10-R (2020) is the high-resolution variant of the M10 rangefinder, featuring a 40.89 MP full-frame BSI-CMOS sensor in the same slim M10 body (33.75 mm thick). The higher resolution and improved dynamic range make it the choice for landscape, studio, and fine-art photographers who demand exceptional detail. The cloth focal-plane shutter and native DNG output are identical to the standard M10.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Leica M10-R

The Leica M10-R does not show shutter count in its on-camera menu. The count is stored in the MakerNote EXIF of every DNG file.

  1. Take any photo with your Leica M10-R and locate the .DNG file on the SD card.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge).
  3. Drag the DNG file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count appears instantly. No upload, no account, no plugins required.

Alternatively: exiftool -ShutterCount image.dng

When buying used: Ask the seller to shoot a fresh DNG in front of you. The M10-R's DNG files are large (~80 MB uncompressed), so have adequate storage ready. Always verify from the original file rather than a screenshot.

M10-R vs M11 — Should You Buy Used?

Resolution comparison

The M10-R offers 40.89 MP while the newer M11 (2022) offers 60 MP. Both use BSI-CMOS sensors. The M11 also introduces triple-resolution DNG capture (18 / 36 / 60 MP binning modes) and USB-C charging. For most practical purposes, the 40.89 MP of the M10-R is more than sufficient — the DNG files are large but manageable, and printing at A1 size (84 × 60 cm) is entirely comfortable.

Price difference and used value

The M10-R commands a lower used price than the M11, making it an attractive entry point into high-resolution Leica M photography. Both cameras accept the same M-mount lenses. If the M11's features (USB-C, in-camera DNG compression options, 60 MP) are not compelling for your workflow, a low-count M10-R represents excellent value.

Lens compatibility: Both the M10-R and M11 accept all Leica M-mount lenses, including Voigtländer and Zeiss alternatives, as well as Leica R lenses via adapter. The M10-R's 40.89 MP sensor puts significant demands on lens resolution — use the best optics available to fully exploit the sensor.

Leica M10-R Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check shutter count on the Leica M10-R?

Drop a DNG file from your M10-R into shuttercount.app. The count is read from EXIF data entirely in your browser — no upload required.

Does the M10-R have an electronic shutter?

No. Like all Leica M digital cameras, the M10-R uses only a mechanical cloth focal-plane shutter. Every exposure increments the shutter count. There is no silent electronic mode.

Is Leica shutter replacement expensive?

Yes. Leica service is significantly more expensive than Japanese camera brands. Budget €500–1,000+ for a shutter replacement through an authorised Leica service centre, plus additional costs for any other servicing found during inspection.

What size are Leica M10-R DNG files?

Uncompressed DNGs from the M10-R are typically 75–85 MB per file. Leica also offers compressed DNG recording (losslessly compressed), reducing file sizes to approximately 40–50 MB. Factor storage capacity into your workflow when evaluating used bodies.
