Fujifilm X-M5 — Shutter Rating

The Fujifilm X-M5 (2024) is Fujifilm's most compact X-mount interchangeable-lens camera, reviving the X-M line after a long hiatus. It features a 26.1 MP X-Trans CMOS 5 sensor, the latest X Processor 5, and a fully articulated rear LCD optimised for vlogging and self-shooting. Despite its compact form factor, the X-M5 supports 6.2K video and multiple film simulations. Fujifilm does not publish an official shutter rating; the estimated lifespan is approximately 150,000 actuations.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Fujifilm X-M5

The Fujifilm X-M5 stores the image counter in MakerNote tag 0x1438 of every RAF RAW file. ShutterCount reads it automatically.

  1. Take any photo with your Fujifilm X-M5 and locate the .RAF file on your memory card or computer.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge).
  3. Drag the RAF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The shutter count and estimated life percentage appear instantly — no upload, no account required.

How Does ShutterCount Read the X-M5 Shutter Count?

Fujifilm's RAF format wraps a standard TIFF/EXIF block. ShutterCount parses the RAF header to locate the embedded EXIF segment, then reads the Fujifilm MakerNote IFD. The shutter counter is stored as a 32-bit value in tag 0x1438, consistent across modern Fujifilm bodies including the X-M5.

Known Fujifilm quirk — counter reset: The image counter in RAF tag 0x1438 may reset to zero after the camera's memory card is formatted. The physical shutter count inside the camera is unaffected, but the counter readable from RAF files may restart. Always verify from a very recent RAF file.

Does video recording wear the shutter?

No. Video recording on the X-M5 uses the electronic readout from the sensor and does not engage the mechanical shutter. The shutter count only increments for mechanical stills exposures. For a primarily video-focused camera like the X-M5, the mechanical shutter count is likely to be very low relative to total hours of use.

Fujifilm X-M5 — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Fujifilm X-M5?

Shoot a RAF RAW file, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from MakerNote tag 0x1438 entirely in your browser — no upload needed.

Is there an official rated shutter life for the X-M5?

No. Fujifilm does not publish an official shutter actuation rating for the X-M5. The ~150,000 estimate is based on the camera's compact construction and market tier. Higher-tier bodies like the X-T5 carry an official 300,000-actuation rating.

How does the X-M5 compare to the X-S20 for used-camera buyers?

The X-S20 (2023) and X-M5 (2024) target similar content-creator audiences, but differ in form factor. The X-S20 has a larger grip, IBIS, and a mode dial, while the X-M5 is more compact and lightweight without IBIS. Both use the same 26.1 MP sensor. For stills photography where shutter count matters more, the X-S20's IBIS and grip are practical advantages.

Can I check from a JPEG?

Fujifilm does not reliably embed the shutter counter in JPEG files. Use a .RAF file for accurate results.
