How Does ShutterCount Read the ZV-E10 Shutter Count?

The Sony ZV-E10 stores the shutter count encrypted in ARW MakerNote tag 0x9050. ShutterCount applies Sony's cubic residue decryption (modulo 249) and reads the count from the appropriate byte offset. The ZV-E10 shares the same MakerNote structure as other Sony APS-C bodies of its generation.

All processing happens locally in your browser. The ARW file is never transmitted anywhere.

Sony ZV-E10 Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Sony ZV-E10?

Shoot an ARW file, then drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is decrypted from tag 0x9050 in your browser.

What is the rated shutter life of the Sony ZV-E10?

Sony rates the ZV-E10 at 200,000 actuations — the same as the A6400 and A6600.

Does video recording affect the shutter count?

No. Video recording does not engage the mechanical shutter. The count only reflects still photo captures. For a video-centric camera like the ZV-E10, the shutter count tells only part of the story.

Can the Sony ZV-E10 shutter count be reset or faked?

Only a Sony authorised service centre can reset the hardware counter. Always verify from an original ARW file, not a screenshot.
