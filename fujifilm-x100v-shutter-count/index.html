Fujifilm X100V — Shutter Rating & Compact Context

The Fujifilm X100V (2020) became one of the most sought-after compact cameras in recent memory — a cultural phenomenon driven by its retro design, exceptional 26.1 MP X-Trans CMOS 4 sensor, fixed 23mm f/2 lens (35mm equivalent), and Fujifilm's beloved film simulations. It features both a mechanical leaf shutter (rated at approximately 300,000 actuations) and an electronic shutter for silent shooting. The tilting touchscreen LCD, weather resistance (with adapter ring and filter), and hybrid OVF/EVF make it a remarkably capable street, travel, and everyday camera.

Leaf shutter advantage: The X100V uses a leaf shutter integrated into the lens rather than a focal-plane shutter. Leaf shutters are generally more durable and have higher rated lifespans. The ~300,000-actuation rating reflects this durability. Electronic shutter shots do not count toward the mechanical total.

How to Check Shutter Count on the Fujifilm X100V

  1. Take a still photo with your Fujifilm X100V and locate the .RAF file.
  2. Open shuttercount.app in any modern browser.
  3. Drag the RAF file onto the drop zone, or click to open a file picker.
  4. The image count appears instantly, read from MakerNote tag 0x1438 in your browser.

If you shoot in electronic shutter mode or JPEG only, switch to mechanical shutter and RAF for the most accurate reading.

How Does ShutterCount Read the Fujifilm X100V Shutter Count?

Fujifilm stores the image counter in RAF files as MakerNote tag 0x1438 (ImageCount). ShutterCount reads this tag directly from the RAF file in your browser. Note that this counter may reset if the camera's internal storage is formatted — for the most reliable assessment, check the count from a recently taken RAF file.

Fujifilm X100V Shutter Count — FAQ

How do I check the shutter count of my Fujifilm X100V?

Shoot a RAF frame with the mechanical shutter and drop it into shuttercount.app. The count is read from tag 0x1438 in your browser, instantly and privately.

What is the rated shutter life of the Fujifilm X100V?

The X100V's leaf shutter is rated at approximately 300,000 actuations. Leaf shutters tend to be more reliable than focal-plane shutters at high counts.

X100V vs X100VI — is the V still worth buying?

The X100V remains an excellent camera. Its 26.1 MP sensor, film simulations, and fixed 23mm f/2 lens deliver outstanding results. The X100VI adds IBIS and a 40 MP sensor, but the X100V at used-market prices represents exceptional value for street, travel, and everyday photography.

Does the electronic shutter affect the count?

No. Electronic shutter shots do not increment the mechanical shutter counter. Only mechanical shutter actuations are counted in tag 0x1438.
